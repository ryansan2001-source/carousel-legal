Carousel Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 17, 2026

Carousel is a web browser for iPhone. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information. Short version: Carousel does not collect, store, or share your personal data.

Information we collect

None. Carousel has no account system, no login, and includes no analytics or third-party tracking software. We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal information.

Information that stays on your device

Everything you do in Carousel stays on your device, with one optional exception: iCloud sync, described below:

  • Your sites and carousels: the websites you add and how you group them are stored locally on your device (and, if you use iCloud, synced through your own iCloud account, see "iCloud sync" below).
  • Browsing data: cookies, logins, history, and website storage are managed by Apple's WebKit and kept on your device, the same as in Safari. We do not read or transmit this data.
  • Private Browsing: when enabled, pages load in a temporary session that is discarded and leaves nothing on disk.
  • Clear data on exit: when enabled, website data is wiped when you close the app.

Purchases

Carousel offers a one-time in-app purchase, Carousel Pro, that unlocks additional features. Payment is processed entirely by Apple through the App Store; Carousel never sees or stores your payment details. Whether you own the unlock is determined on your device through Apple's StoreKit, and that information is not transmitted to us.

iCloud sync

If you're signed into iCloud, the carousels you create (their names and the website addresses you add) are synced through your own iCloud account so they're available on your other devices. This information is stored in your personal iCloud space provided by Apple; it is not sent to us, and we cannot access it. You can turn this off at any time by disabling iCloud for Carousel in your device settings. Syncing covers your carousels only, not your browsing data, logins, or cookies, which always stay on the device where they're created.

Website icons

To show an icon next to each site, Carousel requests that site's icon (/favicon.ico) directly from the site's own server. We do not use a third-party icon service, so your list of sites is never sent to us or any other company.

The websites you visit

Carousel is a browser, so it loads web pages you choose to open. Those websites have their own privacy practices and may collect information about you according to their own policies. Carousel's built-in ad and cookie-banner blocking can reduce some of this, but the sites you visit are governed by their own policies, not this one.

Children

Carousel provides access to the open web and is rated 17+. It is not directed to children.
